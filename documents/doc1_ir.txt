Information Retrieval (IR) is the science of searching for documents, information within documents, and metadata about documents. It involves building systems that allow users to search collections of unstructured text data. An IR system must process document text, build efficient indexes, and compute similarity scores to rank results. The core of any classic search engine is the inverted index, which maps vocabulary terms to the documents they appear in. Modern retrieval systems use techniques such as vector space models, TF-IDF weighting, and neural ranking. Tolerant retrieval, which handles spellings and wildcards, is essential for a robust search experience.
